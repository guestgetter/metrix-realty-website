Key Market Trends Shaping 2025-2026

Interest Rates and Financing

Current mortgage rates at 3.89% represent significant improvement from peak levels. New programs including 30-year amortization for first-time buyers and increased insurance limits to $1.5 million particularly benefit our markets where average prices align with these thresholds.

Buyer Behavior Shift: Instead of rushing to buy, today's buyers are taking time to research and negotiate. This creates opportunities for well-prepared buyers and realistically-priced sellers.

Seasonal Pattern Changes

Traditional spring market surges have been replaced by more consistent year-round activity. This means:

  • For Buyers: Less competition and more time to evaluate options
  • For Sellers: Good opportunities exist throughout the year, not just spring
  • For Everyone: More predictable market conditions and less pressure to rush decisions

What Buyers Want Now

Buyer preferences have evolved significantly:

  • Energy Efficiency: Solar panels, smart thermostats, and efficient appliances add value
  • Home Offices: Dedicated workspace remains essential for remote workers
  • Multi-Generational Features: In-law suites and flexible living spaces in high demand
  • Outdoor Space: Gardens, patios, and proximity to parks increasingly important

Long-Term Outlook and Projections

Population Growth Supporting Demand

Southwestern Ontario expects 41% population growth by 2051, with London leading at 56.7% growth. This demographic foundation supports sustained housing demand despite short-term market fluctuations.

Economic Development Drivers

  • London: Healthcare expansion, university growth, tech sector development
  • Guelph: Manufacturing, agriculture technology, proximity to innovation corridors
  • Windsor: Automotive renaissance, cross-border commerce, infrastructure investment
