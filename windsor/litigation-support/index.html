When Is a Litigation Appraisal Required in Windsor?

Legal and dispute matters involving real property in Windsor-Essex frequently require an independent, CUSPAP-compliant appraisal prepared by a qualified professional.

Matrimonial & Divorce

Independent property valuations for equalization of net family property under the Family Law Act. Serving Windsor-area lawyers, mediators, and courts requiring defensible property value opinions at the date of separation.

Estate & Date-of-Death Valuations

Retrospective appraisals prepared as of the date of death for estate administration, probate, CRA compliance, and equalization among beneficiaries. Accepted by estate trustees, lawyers, and accountants.

Expropriation Matters

Compensation valuations for expropriation proceedings under the Expropriations Act. Windsor-Essex has active infrastructure development — including the Gordie Howe International Bridge corridor — requiring experienced expropriation appraisal support.

Shareholder & Partnership Disputes

Independent valuations for commercial and investment property disputes between business partners, shareholders, or co-owners requiring a neutral, defensible property value opinion.

Property Tax Assessment Appeals

Appraisal evidence supporting MPAC assessment appeals before the Assessment Review Board for commercial, industrial, and investment properties in Windsor and Essex County.

Commercial Property Disputes

Court-ready valuations for commercial litigation matters including lease disputes, insurance claims, construction liens, and mortgage enforcement proceedings in Windsor-area courts.

Litigation Appraisals in Windsor-Essex: What Makes This Market Different

Windsor-Essex has a distinct commercial and industrial property market shaped by factors that require local knowledge to value accurately in a legal context. An appraiser unfamiliar with the Windsor market can produce a report that is technically compliant but factually incorrect.

Key Windsor-Essex considerations that affect litigation appraisals:

  • →Gordie Howe Bridge Corridor: Expropriation and proximity impacts along the new international crossing require familiarity with Windsor's infrastructure development and the Expropriations Act process.
  • →Automotive Industrial Values: Industrial and manufacturing properties tied to the Stellantis and Ford supply chain have unique market characteristics that general appraisers may not understand.
  • →Essex County Agricultural Land: Rural and agricultural properties in Essex, Leamington, and Kingsville require separate market expertise distinct from urban Windsor valuations.
  • →Cross-Border Considerations: Some Windsor commercial properties have values influenced by proximity to the US border — a factor rarely considered outside of Windsor appraisals.
