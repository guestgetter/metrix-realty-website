General Appraisal Questions

A real estate appraisal is an unbiased professional opinion of a property's market value. It's conducted by a licensed appraiser who analyzes the property, comparable sales, market conditions, and other factors to determine what a willing buyer would pay a willing seller in an arm's length transaction.

Appraisals are required for various purposes including:

  • Mortgage financing and refinancing
  • Estate planning and probate
  • Property tax assessment appeals
  • Divorce and matrimonial proceedings
  • Insurance claims
  • Investment analysis
  • Legal disputes and litigation support

The timeline varies depending on the property type and complexity:

  • Residential properties: Typically 3-5 business days
  • Commercial properties: 1-3 weeks depending on complexity
  • Specialized properties: 2-4 weeks

Rush services may be available for urgent requests with additional fees.

Several factors influence property value:

  • Location: Neighborhood, proximity to amenities, schools, transportation
  • Property characteristics: Size, age, condition, layout, features
  • Market conditions: Supply and demand, economic factors
  • Comparable sales: Recent sales of similar properties
  • Zoning and land use: Development potential and restrictions
  • Environmental factors: Flood zones, contamination, natural hazards

Appraisal fees vary based on several factors:

We provide detailed fee quotes upfront with no hidden costs. Contact us for a personalized quote based on your specific needs.

To begin the appraisal process, we typically need:

  • Property address and legal description
  • Purpose of the appraisal
  • Client contact information
  • Property access arrangements
  • Any relevant property documents (deed, survey, etc.)
  • Special considerations or requirements

Our team will guide you through the information gathering process.

While not always required, having someone present during the inspection can be helpful to:

  • Provide access to all areas of the property
  • Answer questions about recent improvements
  • Point out unique features or characteristics
  • Discuss any property issues or concerns

For commercial properties, having a knowledgeable representative present is often beneficial.

After the property inspection, our appraiser will:

  1. Research comparable sales and market data
  2. Analyze the property using appropriate valuation methods
  3. Prepare a comprehensive written report
  4. Review and quality check the report
  5. Deliver the final appraisal report to the client

We're available to discuss the findings and answer any questions about the report.
