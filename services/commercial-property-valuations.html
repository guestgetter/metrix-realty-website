When You Need Us

When Is a Commercial Property Valuation Required?

Commercial property valuations serve critical roles in financing, investment decisions, legal proceedings, and strategic planning. Here are the most common scenarios.

Financing & Refinancing

Lenders require independent appraisals before approving commercial mortgages, construction loans, or refinancing applications. Our reports meet all major Canadian financial institution requirements.

Who needs this: Property owners, investors, developers seeking financing

Acquisition & Disposition

Whether buying or selling, an independent valuation ensures you are making informed decisions based on current market conditions and property-specific factors.

Who needs this: Buyers, sellers, investment firms, REITs

Portfolio Management

Institutional investors, pension funds, and property managers require regular valuations for financial reporting, asset management, and strategic planning purposes.

Who needs this: Institutional investors, pension funds, asset managers

Financial Reporting & Tax

IFRS and ASPE compliance often requires fair value measurement of real estate assets. Our valuations support annual reporting, tax filings, and property tax appeals.

Who needs this: Corporations, accountants, tax advisors

Insurance Purposes

Accurate valuations ensure commercial properties are properly insured. We provide replacement cost and market value assessments for insurance underwriting and claims.

Who needs this: Property owners, insurance companies, risk managers

Legal & Partnership Disputes

Partnership dissolutions, shareholder disputes, and commercial litigation often require independent valuations that can withstand legal scrutiny and cross-examination.

Who needs this: Lawyers, business partners, shareholders

Our Valuation Approach

1

Income Approach

Direct capitalization and discounted cash flow analysis based on actual and market rental data

2

Direct Comparison Approach

Analysis of comparable sales with adjustments for property-specific characteristics

3

Cost Approach

Replacement or reproduction cost analysis for specialized or newly constructed properties

A professional appraisal is an independent, unbiased opinion of value completed by an accredited appraiser following CUSPAP standards. It involves a thorough analysis using recognized valuation methodologies and is accepted by lenders, courts, and government agencies. A broker's opinion of value (BOV) is an informal estimate typically prepared by a real estate agent and is not accepted for financing, legal, or regulatory purposes.
