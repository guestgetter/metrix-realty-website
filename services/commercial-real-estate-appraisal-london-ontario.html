What Is a Commercial Appraisal?

What Is a Commercial Real Estate Appraisal and When Do You Need One?

A commercial real estate appraisal is a certified, written opinion of a property's market value prepared by a qualified appraiser under CUSPAP standards. In London and Southwestern Ontario, these reports are required whenever the value of income-producing or non-residential property must be established for a formal purpose: financing, sale, legal proceedings, or regulatory compliance.

Unlike a broker's opinion of value, a CUSPAP-compliant appraisal is prepared independently, documents the appraiser's full methodology, and is signed by a designated professional. Lenders, courts, and government bodies rely on this independence. The report form, scope of work, and applicable approaches to value are determined by the assignment conditions and intended use — not by a preferred outcome.

Common triggers for a commercial appraisal in the London market include new mortgage financing or refinancing, acquisition or disposition of an investment property, estate or probate settlement, matrimonial separation, property tax appeal before the Assessment Review Board, expropriation, and litigation requiring expert testimony. Each intended use may affect which approaches to value are applied and how the report is scoped.

Intended Uses

Common Reasons to Commission a Commercial Appraisal

The intended use of an appraisal shapes its scope, approaches, and report form. Below are the most common scenarios we handle for clients in London and across the region.

Financing & Refinancing

Lenders require an independent appraisal before advancing funds on commercial mortgages, construction loans, or refinancing transactions. The report must be prepared by a qualified appraiser and meet the lender's scope requirements.

Acquisition & Disposition

An independent market value opinion helps buyers and sellers negotiate from an informed position and supports due diligence requirements for institutional purchasers.

Estate & Probate

Date-of-death valuations for estate administration, probate filings, and CRA capital gains reporting. Retrospective appraisals are a core part of our practice.

Matrimonial & Divorce

Under Ontario's Family Law Act, commercial property owned by either spouse may need to be valued at a specific date to calculate net family property. Our reports are prepared in a format accepted by Ontario family courts.

Litigation & Expert Support

Our appraisers provide court-ready reports and expert witness testimony for partnership disputes, shareholder buyouts, expropriation matters, and insurance claims. See our litigation support page for detail.

Property Tax Appeal

A market value appraisal can provide evidence in support of a Request for Reconsideration or an ARB appeal of an MPAC assessment. We do not offer legal or tax advice; clients should consult appropriate professionals on appeal strategy.

What Documents Help Us Provide a Quote?

Having key documents ready before contacting us helps us scope the assignment accurately and provide a meaningful fee estimate. We can often proceed with a subset of these — feel free to reach out with whatever you have available.

  • Municipal address and legal description or PIN
  • Current rent roll and lease abstracts (income-producing properties)
  • Recent operating statements (2-3 years, if available)
  • Current MPAC assessment notice
  • Building plans, floor plans, or site plan (if available)
  • Intended use of the appraisal and any lender or legal requirements
  • Any recent purchase and sale agreement or listing information
Our Process

How a Commercial Appraisal Works

1

Consultation and Scope Definition

We discuss your requirements, confirm the intended use, identify the appropriate effective date, and provide a clear fee and timeline estimate. Scope of work is agreed before any inspection is scheduled.

2

Property Inspection

An on-site inspection documents physical condition, building measurements, improvements, tenancy, and all factors that influence value. We collect relevant documents from owners or managers during this stage.

3

Market Research and Analysis

We research comparable sales, lease transactions, cap rates, and market conditions using CoStar Professional and other verified data sources. The applicable approaches to value — sales comparison, income, or cost — are applied based on the property type and assignment conditions.

4

Report Delivery

A detailed, professionally formatted appraisal report compliant with CUSPAP 2026 is delivered within the agreed timeline. Contact us to discuss your schedule requirements.
