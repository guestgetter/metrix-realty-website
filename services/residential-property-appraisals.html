When You Need Us

When Is a Residential Appraisal Required?

A professional home appraisal provides an independent, unbiased opinion of your property's market value. Here are the most common situations where an appraisal is needed.

Mortgage & Purchase Financing

Lenders require an independent appraisal before approving a mortgage to ensure the property's value supports the loan amount. Our reports are accepted by all major Canadian banks and credit unions.

Who needs this: Homebuyers, lenders, mortgage brokers

Refinancing & HELOC

When refinancing your mortgage or applying for a Home Equity Line of Credit, an up-to-date appraisal confirms current market value and the equity available in your property.

Who needs this: Homeowners seeking refinancing or equity access

Estate Planning & Probate

Accurate property valuations are essential for estate settlement, calculating estate taxes, determining fair distribution among beneficiaries, and satisfying CRA reporting requirements.

Who needs this: Executors, estate lawyers, beneficiaries

Divorce & Matrimonial Property

Family law proceedings require independent valuations to determine equalization payments and fair property division. Our court-ready reports meet all Ontario family court requirements.

Who needs this: Family lawyers, separating couples

Property Tax Appeals

If you believe your MPAC assessment is too high, a professional appraisal provides the evidence needed to support an appeal before the Assessment Review Board (ARB).

Who needs this: Homeowners disputing MPAC assessments

Relocation & Pre-Listing

Know your home's true market value before listing. A professional appraisal helps set realistic expectations and supports corporate relocation programs requiring independent valuations.

Who needs this: Homeowners, real estate agents, employers

Frequently Asked Questions

Standard residential appraisals are typically completed within 2-5 business days from the date of inspection. Rush reports can be accommodated within 24-48 hours for straightforward assignments. Timeline depends on property complexity and current volume.

Our appraisers assess overall condition, square footage, number of rooms, upgrades and renovations, lot size, location characteristics, neighbourhood amenities, and any factors that may affect value. We photograph the property inside and out and note any deferred maintenance or functional issues.

Fees vary based on property type, location, complexity, and the intended use of the report. We provide transparent, upfront fee quotes after an initial consultation. Contact us for a no-obligation quote specific to your property.

If you are the client who engaged our services, you will receive a copy. For lender-ordered appraisals, the report is delivered to the lending institution, and a copy may be provided to you depending on the lender's policies and applicable privacy regulations.

If you believe relevant information was not considered, you may contact us to discuss. We welcome additional comparable sales data or information about recent improvements. We will review any new information and determine if a revision is warranted.
