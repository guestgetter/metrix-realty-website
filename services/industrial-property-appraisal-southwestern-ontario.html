What Does an Industrial Appraisal Cover?

What Does an Industrial Property Appraisal Involve?

An industrial property appraisal is a certified, independent opinion of market value for a property used in manufacturing, warehousing, distribution, processing, or related industrial activities. Like all CUSPAP-compliant appraisals, the report documents the appraiser's analysis, data sources, and reasoning — and is signed by a designated professional.

Industrial properties present distinct valuation challenges. Clear height, dock and grade loading, electrical service, crane capacity, lot coverage, zoning classification, and proximity to transportation corridors all affect market value in ways that differ from office or retail property. Environmental considerations and remediation history are also relevant factors that appraisers note but do not assess — environmental investigations are the work of qualified environmental professionals.

Our AACI-designated appraisers have completed industrial assignments across Southwestern Ontario for over 30 years. We understand the region's industrial submarkets: from London's established industrial parks to Windsor-Essex manufacturing corridors, Sarnia's petrochemical precinct, and KW-Cambridge's advanced manufacturing base. Local submarket knowledge matters in industrial valuation, and we have it.

Who We Work With

Who Needs an Industrial Property Appraisal?

Industrial appraisals are commissioned by a wide range of clients for financing, legal, and strategic purposes. The intended use shapes the scope and form of the report.

Lenders and Financial Institutions

Commercial mortgage lenders, chartered banks, and credit unions require independent appraisals before advancing funds on industrial property financing or refinancing.

Property Owners and Investors

Owner-occupiers and investors acquiring or disposing of industrial property benefit from an independent value opinion to support negotiations and due diligence.

Manufacturers and Operators

Manufacturing companies valuing owner-occupied facilities for financial reporting, sale-leaseback transactions, insurance, or corporate restructuring.

Lawyers and Legal Proceedings

Litigation matters, expropriation proceedings, partnership disputes, matrimonial separations, and estate administration all may require independent industrial property appraisals.

Municipalities and Government Bodies

Municipal and provincial agencies require independent appraisals for land acquisition, economic development transactions, expropriation, and infrastructure projects involving industrial land.

Property Tax Proceedings

Property owners disputing an MPAC industrial assessment may commission an appraisal as market value evidence for a Request for Reconsideration or ARB appeal. See our property tax appraisal page for guardrails and context.

Valuation Factors

Key Factors in Industrial Property Valuation

Industrial properties are valued based on physical characteristics, functional utility, and market conditions that differ substantially from commercial or residential property. Our appraisers analyze each of these factors in detail.

Clear Height and Usable Bay Area

Clear height directly affects a building's suitability for modern warehouse and distribution uses. Higher clear heights typically command premium value per square foot in the market. Bay spacing and column grid also influence functional utility.

Loading Dock and Grade Doors

The number, type, and configuration of loading positions significantly affect value for distribution and warehousing uses. Grade-level doors, dock-height levellers, and truck court depth are analyzed individually.

Electrical Service and Power Capacity

Electrical service capacity is a critical factor for manufacturing users. Amperage, voltage, and available service from the utility are documented and considered in the appraisal. Heavy industrial uses may require specialized power infrastructure that narrows the buyer pool.

Crane Systems and Structural Load

Overhead cranes and the structural capacity to support them add value for certain manufacturing users. We document crane type, capacity, and span. Properties built for heavy crane use may appeal to a limited market segment.

Zoning, Permitted Uses, and Site Coverage

Zoning classification determines permitted industrial uses and development potential. Lot coverage, setbacks, parking ratios, and outdoor storage permissions all affect the property's functional utility and its appeal to different user types.

Environmental Considerations

Appraisers note visible environmental indicators and disclosed remediation history but do not assess environmental conditions. Where environmental issues are known or suspected, a qualified environmental professional's report is the appropriate reference point. Environmental stigma, if market evidence supports it, is a consideration in the appraisal.

Getting Started

What Documents Are Helpful for an Industrial Appraisal?

Industrial appraisals require information about the physical building and site, as well as the intended use of the appraisal. The list below covers what is typically useful — contact us with whatever you have available.

  • Municipal address, legal description, or PIN
  • Building size (total GLA), clear height, and year of construction
  • Number and type of loading positions (dock-height and grade-level)
  • Electrical service capacity (amps/volts) if known
  • Crane specifications (type, capacity, and span) if applicable
  • Current MPAC assessment notice
  • Lease or tenancy information (if income-producing)
  • Intended use of the appraisal and any lender or legal requirements
Our Process

How an Industrial Appraisal Works

1

Consultation and Scope Agreement

We discuss your property, confirm the intended use, and agree on scope of work, effective date, and timeline before any work begins. A fee estimate is provided upfront.

2

Property Inspection

A thorough on-site inspection documents building dimensions, construction, functional features (clear height, loading, power, cranes), site characteristics, and condition. We collect relevant documents from owners, managers, or occupants during this visit.

3

Market Research and Valuation Analysis

We research comparable industrial sales and lease transactions using CoStar Professional and other data sources. We apply the sales comparison approach, income approach, or cost approach as appropriate to the property type and assignment conditions.

4

Report Delivery

A detailed, CUSPAP 2026-compliant appraisal report is prepared and delivered within the agreed timeline. Contact us to discuss your schedule requirements.
