When You Need Us

When Is Litigation Support Required?

When legal proceedings require expert real estate valuation, our experienced appraisers provide comprehensive opinions and professional testimony that withstand cross-examination.

Matrimonial & Divorce Proceedings

Under Ontario's Family Law Act, the matrimonial home and other real property must be valued at specific dates to calculate net family property and equalization payments. Our valuations are prepared in a format accepted by Ontario family courts.

Who needs this: Family lawyers, separating couples

Expropriation & Land Acquisition

When government or public agencies acquire private property, owners are entitled to fair compensation under Ontario's Expropriations Act. We assess market value, Injurious Affection, and Disturbance Damages to protect property owners' rights.

Who needs this: Property owners, municipalities, lawyers

Partnership & Shareholder Disputes

Business dissolutions, shareholder buyouts, and partnership disputes involving real property require independent valuations that can withstand legal scrutiny and arbitration proceedings.

Who needs this: Business lawyers, partners, shareholders

Estate & Probate Litigation

Contested estates, beneficiary disputes, and challenges to estate valuations require independent appraisals that meet court standards. We provide date-of-death valuations and retrospective assessments.

Who needs this: Estate lawyers, beneficiaries, executors

Property Tax Appeals

If your MPAC property assessment is inaccurate, a professional appraisal provides the evidence needed to support an appeal before the Assessment Review Board (ARB). We prepare detailed reports that address MPAC's methodology.

Who needs this: Property owners, tax lawyers

Insurance Claims & Disputes

Insurance disputes involving property damage, total loss claims, or coverage disagreements require independent valuations to quantify loss and support fair settlement negotiations.

Who needs this: Insurance companies, property owners, adjusters

Frequently Asked Questions

Our appraisers hold the AACI designation (Accredited Appraiser Canadian Institute), the highest professional designation in Canadian real estate appraisal. They have decades of experience providing testimony in Ontario Superior Court, the Assessment Review Board, Ontario Land Tribunal, and various arbitration and mediation proceedings. Their qualifications have been consistently accepted by courts across Ontario.

Litigation appraisals require a higher standard of documentation because they must withstand cross-examination. Reports include detailed methodology explanations, comprehensive comparable analysis, clear reasoning supporting the conclusion, and full disclosure of assumptions. The appraiser must be prepared to defend their opinion under oath.

We frequently provide retrospective valuations for a specific date in the past, commonly required for estate settlements (date of death valuations), matrimonial proceedings (date of marriage and date of separation valuations), and tax matters. These require specialized research into historical market conditions and comparable data.

Under Ontario's Expropriations Act, property owners are entitled to fair compensation when their property is acquired for public purposes. Our appraisers assess market value of the lands taken, Injurious Affection (loss in value to remaining lands), and Disturbance Damages (costs associated with relocation and disruption). We provide independent valuations that protect property owners' rights throughout the process.

Under Ontario's Family Law Act, the matrimonial home and other properties must be valued as of specific dates (typically date of marriage and date of separation) to calculate net family property and equalization payments. We provide independent valuations for both dates, prepared in a format accepted by Ontario family courts, and are available for testimony if required.
